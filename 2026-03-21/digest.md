## News

### AI Security

- **[Trivy Security Scanner GitHub Actions Breached, 75 Tags Hijacked](https://thehackernews.com/2026/03/trivy-security-scanner-github-actions.html)**: Attackers hijacked the `aquasecurity/trivy-action` and `aquasecurity/setup-trivy` GitHub Actions tags to exfiltrate CI/CD secrets — the second compromise of Trivy in under a month.
- **[Widely Used Trivy Scanner Compromised in Ongoing Supply-Chain Attack](https://arstechnica.com/security/2026/03/widely-used-trivy-scanner-compromised-in-ongoing-supply-chain-attack/)**: Ars Technica's reporting urges administrators to treat this as a rotate-your-secrets emergency across all affected pipelines.
- **[Critical Langflow Flaw CVE-2026-33017 Triggers Attacks Within 20 Hours of Disclosure](https://thehackernews.com/2026/03/critical-langflow-flaw-cve-2026-33017.html)**: The CVSS 9.3 flaw in the AI-builder platform combines missing authentication with code injection for unauthenticated RCE; active exploitation began almost immediately after public disclosure.
- **[The Importance of Behavioral Analytics in AI-Enabled Cyber Attacks](https://thehackernews.com/2026/03/the-importance-of-behavioral-analytics.html)**: Cybercriminals are now deploying AI to generate personalized phishing emails, deepfakes, and malware tuned to evade signature-based detection by mimicking legitimate user behavior.
- **[FBI Links Signal Phishing Attacks to Russian Intelligence Services](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/fbi-links-signal-phishing-attacks-to-russian-intelligence-services/)**: Russian intelligence-linked actors are actively targeting Signal and WhatsApp users with phishing campaigns that have already compromised thousands of encrypted-messaging accounts.
- **[Musician Admits to $10M Streaming Royalty Fraud Using AI Bots](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/musician-pleads-guilty-to-10m-streaming-fraud-powered-by-ai-bots/)**: North Carolina musician Michael Smith pleaded guilty to using AI-powered bots to generate fraudulent streams across Spotify, Apple Music, Amazon Music, and YouTube Music.
- **[Patch Now: Oracle's Fusion Middleware Has Critical RCE Flaw](https://www.darkreading.com/vulnerabilities-threats/patch-oracle-fusion-middleware-rce-flaw)**: CVE-2026-21992 allows unauthenticated remote code execution against Oracle Identity Manager and Web Services Manager instances exposed to the internet; Oracle issued an emergency out-of-band patch.
- **[CISA Orders Feds to Patch Max-Severity Cisco Flaw by Sunday](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/cisa-orders-feds-to-patch-max-severity-cisco-flaw-by-sunday/)**: CISA mandated federal agencies patch CVE-2026-20131 in Cisco Secure Firewall Management Center by March 22; the Interlock ransomware gang had exploited the flaw weeks before public disclosure.
- **[Interlock Ransomware Targets Cisco Enterprise Firewalls](https://www.darkreading.com/threat-intelligence/interlock-ransomware-targets-cisco-enterprise-firewalls)**: The double-extortion gang had access to the critical Cisco firewall vulnerability in advance of its public release, indicating possible pre-disclosure zero-day exploitation.
- **[Cyber OpSec Fail: Beast Gang Exposes Ransomware Server](https://www.darkreading.com/threat-intelligence/opsec-beast-gang-exposes-ransomware-server)**: Files on Beast ransomware's exposed cloud server reveal a systematic strategy of targeting network backups as a primary attack tactic.
- **[International Joint Action Disrupts World's Largest DDoS Botnets](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/aisuru-kimwolf-jackskid-and-mossad-botnets-disrupted-in-joint-action/)**: US, German, and Canadian authorities dismantled the C2 infrastructure of the Aisuru, KimWolf, JackSkid, and Mossad IoT botnets in a coordinated international operation.
- **[Proton Mail Shared User Information with the Police](https://www.schneier.com/blog/archives/2026/03/proton-mail-shared-user-information-with-the-police.html)**: Proton Mail provided subscriber payment metadata to Swiss authorities who forwarded it to the FBI — a reminder that even privacy-focused services operate under legal compulsion.
- **[Google Adds 24-Hour Wait for Unverified App Sideloading](https://thehackernews.com/2026/03/google-adds-24-hour-wait-for-unverified.html)**: Android's new "advanced flow" imposes a mandatory cooling-off period before installing apps from unverified developers, aiming to reduce malware and scam exposure.
- **[Magento PolyShell Flaw Enables Unauthenticated Uploads, RCE and Account Takeover](https://thehackernews.com/2026/03/magento-polyshell-flaw-enables.html)**: A critical Magento REST API flaw lets unauthenticated attackers upload disguised malicious executables and take over accounts; no in-the-wild exploitation confirmed yet.

## Key Themes

- **AI infrastructure under attack**: Supply-chain compromises of DevSecOps tooling (Trivy) and near-instant exploitation of AI platform vulnerabilities (Langflow) mark a dangerous escalation in attacks on AI development infrastructure.
- **US AI regulation consolidation**: The Trump administration's federal preemption framework, backed by Big Tech, is moving to eliminate state-level AI laws and centralize authority in Washington.
- **The agentic pivot**: OpenAI's automated researcher, Anthropic's always-on Claude Code, and Nvidia's OpenClaw platform all signal a broad industry shift from AI assistants to autonomous AI agents.
- **AI coding wars intensify**: OpenAI's acquisition of Astral and its superapp consolidation strategy put it in direct competition with Anthropic, Google, and others for dominance in AI-powered developer tooling.
- **Consumer AI backlash**: Microsoft rolling back Copilot features and widespread public distrust of AI highlight the gap between enterprise adoption enthusiasm and actual user sentiment.
