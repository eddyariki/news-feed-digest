## News

### AI Security

- **[OpenAI Codex Security Scanned 1.2 Million Commits and Found 10,561 High-Severity Issues](https://thehackernews.com/2026/03/openai-codex-security-scanned-12.html)** — OpenAI has launched Codex Security, an AI agent that builds deep context about projects to identify, validate, and propose fixes for vulnerabilities. Available in free research preview to ChatGPT Pro, Enterprise, Business, and Edu customers.

- **[Anthropic Finds 22 Firefox Vulnerabilities Using Claude Opus 4.6 AI Model](https://thehackernews.com/2026/03/anthropic-finds-22-firefox.html)** — As part of a security partnership with Mozilla, Anthropic used Claude to discover 22 new browser vulnerabilities over two weeks; 14 were rated high severity. All were patched in Firefox 148.

- **[Microsoft: Hackers Abusing AI at Every Stage of Cyberattacks](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/microsoft-hackers-abusing-ai-at-every-stage-of-cyberattacks/)** — Microsoft reports that threat actors are increasingly integrating AI across all phases of attacks — from reconnaissance to post-exploitation — to accelerate operations and lower the skill bar required.

- **[Termite Ransomware Breaches Linked to ClickFix CastleRAT Attacks](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/termite-ransomware-breaches-linked-to-clickfix-castlerat-attacks/)** — Ransomware group Velvet Tempest is chaining the ClickFix social engineering technique with legitimate Windows utilities to deploy DonutLoader malware and the CastleRAT backdoor.

### USA

- **[OpenAI Robotics Lead Caitlin Kalinowski Quits in Response to Pentagon Deal](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/07/openai-robotics-lead-caitlin-kalinowski-quits-in-response-to-pentagon-deal/)** — Kalinowski, who led OpenAI's hardware and robotics efforts, publicly cited the company's Department of Defense agreement as the reason for her resignation, marking a high-profile internal rupture over AI militarization.

- **[OpenAI Launches Codex for Open Source](https://simonwillison.net/2026/Mar/7/codex-for-open-source/#atom-everything)** — Following Anthropic's offer of six months of free Claude Max to open source maintainers, OpenAI has launched a comparable program providing six months of ChatGPT Pro — including Codex and conditional access to Codex Security — to core contributors of major open source projects.

- **[The OpenClaw Superfan Meetup Serves Optimism and Lobster](https://www.theverge.com/ai-artificial-intelligence/890517/openclaw-clawcon-meetup-nyc-open-source-ai)** — ClawCon, an open-source AI community gathering in Manhattan, drew an enthusiastic crowd of open source AI advocates, reflecting grassroots enthusiasm in the broader AI ecosystem.

### Japan (AI & Tech)

- **[NEC, Tokyo University, and NTT Integrate AI Agent Technology on 6G/IOWN Infrastructure](https://www.itmedia.co.jp/enterprise/articles/2603/07/news016.html)** — The three organizations announced a joint integration of AI agent-oriented technologies on the 6G/IOWN platform, achieving real-time AR support with confirmed latency reduction and sustained precision in large-scale data transmission.

- **[Japan's Digital Agency to Begin Testing AI Use for Administrative Tasks in May](https://www.japantimes.co.jp/news/2026/03/07/japan/digital-agency-ai-test-may/)** — Japan's Digital Agency plans to launch a generative AI pilot in May to assess the effects on work style and task efficiency, sorting out issues related to administrative use before broader deployment.

- **[Nippon Life Insurance's U.S. Arm Sues OpenAI Over Legal Assistance Provided by ChatGPT](https://japannews.yomiuri.co.jp/business/companies/20260307-315041/)** — A U.S. subsidiary of Nippon Life has filed suit against OpenAI, alleging damages stemming from legal guidance that ChatGPT provided to another party in a dispute involving the insurer.

- **[Rohm Shares Soar Most Since 2000 on Denso Acquisition Bid](https://www.japantimes.co.jp/business/2026/03/07/rohm-shares-soar-denso-bid/)** — Japanese chipmaker Rohm surged 18% — its largest single-day gain in 26 years — after receiving an acquisition proposal from automotive supplier Denso, signaling continued consolidation in Japan's semiconductor sector.

- **[Nintendo Sues U.S. Government Over Reciprocal Tariffs](https://www.japantimes.co.jp/business/2026/03/07/nintendo-lawsuit-us-tariffs/)** — Nintendo has taken legal action against Trump administration tariff policies after being forced to delay preorder sales for the Nintendo Switch 2 in the United States.

- **[Children and Social Media: Japan to Debate Pros and Cons of Regulations](https://japannews.yomiuri.co.jp/editorial/yomiuri-editorial/20260307-315047/)** — As overseas momentum builds for restricting minors' access to social media, a Yomiuri editorial calls on Japan to begin a comprehensive domestic debate on whether similar regulations are needed.

- **[Nearly 70% of Japanese Suspect Disinformation Is Spreading Online](https://www.japantimes.co.jp/news/2026/03/07/japan/japan-disinformation-online-spread/)** — A new survey finds growing concern among the Japanese public about foreign manipulation of online information, reinforcing calls for policy responses to AI-enabled disinformation.

## Key Themes

- **AI as a dual-use security tool**: The same week Anthropic used Claude to proactively find 22 browser vulnerabilities, Microsoft documented adversaries using AI at every phase of cyberattacks — the offense-defense arms race is accelerating.
- **OpenAI's military pivot draws internal backlash**: The resignation of the robotics lead over the Pentagon deal is a rare, public signal of ethical friction inside a frontier AI lab.
- **Japan's semiconductor sector consolidating**: The Denso–Rohm bid, combined with NEC/NTT/Tokyo University's 6G/IOWN AI infrastructure push, suggests Japan is actively reorganizing its tech-industrial base around AI infrastructure.
- **AI governance pressure mounts in Japan**: From the Digital Agency's AI pilot to social media regulation debates and disinformation concerns, Japan is moving — cautiously but tangibly — toward AI and platform governance frameworks.
- **Open source AI gaining institutional support**: Both Anthropic and OpenAI now offer substantial free tiers to open source maintainers, reflecting strategic interest in shaping the open-source AI ecosystem.
