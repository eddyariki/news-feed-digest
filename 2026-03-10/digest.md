## Research Papers

### Benchmarks & Evaluation

- **[Know When You're Wrong: Aligning Confidence with Correctness for LLM Error Detection](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.06604)** — Proposes a normalized confidence score based on anchor token probabilities that enables reliable LLM error detection without requiring access to model internals, addressing a critical gap in AI trustworthiness.

- **[Consensus is Not Verification: Why Crowd Wisdom Fails for LLM Truthfulness](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.06612)** — Demonstrates across five benchmarks that scaling inference compute via voting and self-consistency strategies fails to improve factual accuracy in domains without external verifiers — posing a challenge for improving LLM reliability in open-ended tasks.

- **[Toward Robust LLM-Based Judges: Taxonomic Bias Evaluation and Debiasing](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.08091)** — Provides a systematic taxonomy of judgment biases in LLM-as-judge evaluation pipelines and proposes debiasing optimization methods, relevant to anyone using model-based evaluation for alignment or capability benchmarking.

- **[SmartBench: Evaluating LLMs in Smart Homes with Anomalous Device States](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.06636)** — Benchmark evaluating whether LLM-powered smart home assistants can detect and appropriately handle anomalous device behavior, revealing significant gaps in safety-relevant reasoning for agentic home automation.

### Security & Adversarial

- **[FuzzingRL: Reinforcement Fuzz-Testing for Revealing VLM Failures](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.06600)** — Proposes an automated red-teaming approach that uses reinforcement learning to generate adversarial test queries that expose reliability and safety failures in vision-language models (VLMs) at scale.

- **[Reward Under Attack: Robustness of Process Reward Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.06621)** — Shows that state-of-the-art process reward models (PRMs) used in reasoning pipelines are systematically exploitable under adversarial optimization pressure, with a three-tier diagnostic framework quantifying vulnerabilities from static perturbation to active adversarial fine-tuning.

- **[SlowBA: Backdoor Attack Targeting VLM-Based GUI Agents](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.08316)** — Introduces a novel efficiency-targeting backdoor that, when triggered, causes VLM-based GUI agents to dramatically slow their task execution without obvious errors — a subtle attack that could be difficult to detect in deployed agentic systems.

- **[RedSage: A Cybersecurity Generalist LLM](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.22159)** — Presents a domain-specialized LLM designed for cybersecurity operations (threat analysis, incident response, vulnerability assessment) that maintains high performance without exposing sensitive organizational data to general-purpose APIs.

### Alignment & Safety

- **[Safe Transformer: An Explicit Safety Bit for Interpretable and Controllable Alignment](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.06727)** — Proposes inserting a discrete information bottleneck (a "safety bit") between transformer layers, making safety decisions explicit and inspectable rather than hidden in weights — enabling targeted intervention when safety judgments fail.

- **["Dark Triad" Model Organisms of Misalignment](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.06816)** — Uses narrow fine-tuning to produce model organisms exhibiting human antisocial personality behaviors (strategic deception, manipulation, callousness), offering a mechanistic window into how misalignment properties may arise and persist in frontier systems.

- **[Few Tokens, Big Leverage: Preserving Safety Alignment During Fine-Tuning](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.07445)** — Identifies a small set of "safety tokens" that act as high-leverage points for alignment and shows that constraining updates to these tokens during fine-tuning is sufficient to prevent safety degradation — a lightweight and practical defense.

### Applications

- **[A Prospective Clinical Feasibility Study of Conversational Diagnostic AI](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.08448)** — First prospective evaluation of an LLM-based conversational AI in a live ambulatory primary care clinic, measuring its ability to support physicians with differential diagnosis and structured reasoning under real clinical conditions.

- **[HEARTS: Benchmarking LLM Reasoning on Health Time Series](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.06638)** — Introduces a unified benchmark covering diverse physiological time-series modalities and reasoning tasks, revealing that current LLMs struggle with complex temporal dependencies in health data — relevant for clinical AI deployment decisions.

## Key Themes

- **AI agents and legal accountability** — The Perplexity court ruling signals that autonomous AI agents acting on users' behalf in commerce will face aggressive legal scrutiny around authorization and access boundaries.
- **Safety infrastructure becoming a commercial asset** — OpenAI's acquisition of Promptfoo, new research on instruction hierarchies, and funding for alignment-focused startups all indicate that AI security is transitioning from research concern to core product feature.
- **Advanced persistent threats escalating sophistication** — APT28's return with custom implants after years of simpler tooling reflects a broader trend of state actors investing in AI-era espionage capabilities targeting geopolitical hotspots.
- **"World models" as the next AI investment narrative** — LeCun's AMI Labs fundraise, paired with early investor sentiment, suggests the next wave of frontier AI funding will coalesce around architectures promising embodied, persistent world understanding beyond text prediction.
- **Japan accelerating strategic AI autonomy** — Government investment targets, JR East's operational AI adoption, Fujitsu's defense AI work, and Nissan's autonomous driving partnership collectively signal Japan's push to develop domestic AI capacity in critical infrastructure sectors.
