## Fact-Check Report — 2026-02-24

### Part 1: News Digest

**Verdict:** PASS WITH NOTES

#### Issues Found

**[Meta closes $100B AMD chip deal]** — The Highlights section (and the article-level summary) describes the equity component as "an unusual 160-million-share **equity stake**." The TechCrunch source clearly calls it a "160 million-share **warrant**." A warrant is an option to purchase shares, not a current ownership stake; the two are not equivalent. Correction: replace "equity stake" with "warrant" in Highlights; remove the clause "— an unusual equity stake" from the article summary.

**[IR³: Contrastive Inverse RL for Interpretable Reward Hacking Detection]** — The title as listed in the Research Papers section abbreviates and truncates the actual title. Source title (JSON and PDF): *IR³: Contrastive Inverse Reinforcement Learning for Interpretable Detection and **Mitigation** of Reward Hacking*. The digest drops "and Mitigation," changing the described scope of the paper. Correction: restore full title including "Reinforcement Learning" and "and Mitigation."

**[Sycophantic Chatbots Cause Delusional Spiraling, Even in Ideal Bayesians]** (Research Papers digest blurb) — Attributed to "Researchers from MIT and Harvard." Per the PDF, the four authors are affiliated with MIT CSAIL, the University of Washington (Seattle), and MIT Brain & Cognitive Sciences. Harvard is not listed as an affiliation for any author. Correction: "MIT and Harvard" → "MIT and the University of Washington."

### Overall Summary

Both artifacts are largely accurate and well-sourced. Two independent instances of the same error appear — the Sycophantic Chatbots paper is wrongly attributed to "MIT and Harvard" in both the digest blurb and the paper summary, when one author (Max Kleiman-Weiner) is at the University of Washington, not Harvard. A second factual error in the digest mislabels a share warrant as an "equity stake" in both the Highlights and the Meta/AMD article summary; the TechCrunch source consistently uses the term "warrant." A third issue is a truncated paper title for IR³ that omits the word "Mitigation," narrowing the described scope. All three errors have been corrected inline. News URLs, source attributions, headline paraphrases, and all other numerical claims in the paper summaries (benchmark scores, correlation values, participant counts) were verified against source data and PDFs and are accurate.
